Data Privacy & Cybersecurity

In today's digital age, your personal information is constantly at risk. Every online transaction, social media interaction, and digital communication creates data that can be stolen, misused, or sold without your consent.

Data breaches have become increasingly common, affecting millions of consumers annually. When companies fail to protect your sensitive information, they must be held accountable through legal action.

Our firm specializes in privacy class action lawsuits, fighting for consumers whose personal data has been compromised through corporate negligence or deliberate misconduct.

Claims Involving Stolen Private Information

When your personal data is compromised, you have legal rights. Privacy class action lawsuits address violations including:

Types of Privacy Violations

  • Unauthorized data collection - Companies gathering personal information without proper consent
  • Data security failures - Inadequate protection leading to breaches and identity theft
  • Illegal data sharing - Selling or sharing personal information without authorization
  • Surveillance violations - Unauthorized monitoring of personal communications or activities
  • Biometric data misuse - Improper collection or storage of fingerprints, facial recognition data

What is a Data Breach?

A data breach occurs when sensitive, protected, or confidential information is accessed, disclosed, or stolen by unauthorized individuals or entities. This can include:

Common Types of Compromised Data

  • Social Security numbers and government identification
  • Credit card numbers and financial account information
  • Medical records and health insurance information
  • Personal addresses, phone numbers, and email addresses
  • Login credentials and passwords
  • Biometric data (fingerprints, facial recognition)

Harm to Consumers from Data Breaches

Data breaches can cause significant financial and personal harm to consumers, including:

Identity theft and fraud: Criminals use stolen personal information to open fraudulent accounts, make unauthorized purchases, or file false tax returns.

Financial losses: Direct monetary losses from fraudulent transactions, plus costs for credit monitoring, identity restoration services, and legal fees.

Credit damage: Identity theft can severely damage your credit score, affecting your ability to obtain loans, credit cards, or even employment.

Time and stress: Victims spend countless hours resolving identity theft issues, contacting financial institutions, and restoring their good name.

Privacy invasion: Once personal information is compromised, it can be sold on the dark web and used for years of continued harassment and fraud.
